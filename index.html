About

I hold a Ph.D. in Political Science and an M.S. in Social Data Analytics and Research from The University of Texas at Dallas. My research examines how political identities, framing, gender, ideology, and institutional conditions shape public attitudes toward climate change and environmental policy.

Research

Climate Change and Public Opinion

My research investigates how individuals understand climate change and how framing influences concern, perceived seriousness, and support for climate policy.

Gender and Political Attitudes

I examine gender differences in climate attitudes and how political ideology, partisanship, identity, and risk perception shape these differences.

Human Rights Framing

This project studies whether presenting climate change as a human rights issue changes perceptions of urgency and policy priority.

Environmental Politics in the Rio Grande Valley

My regional research focuses on water scarcity, drought, infrastructure, economic development, and public opinion in South Texas.
